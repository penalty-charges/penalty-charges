Challenging a Penalty Charge
When you can challenge a penalty charge

  1. You are entitled to NHS free prescriptions

    2. You might like to refer to the other side of this leaflet to help you work out if you are entitled to free NHS prescriptions.

  2. You have a valid in date certificate

    3. An example of a certificate that entitles you to free NHS prescriptions is a HC2 form. These are for people on low incomes.

  3. You have a prepayment certificate

    4. These certificates are evidence that you have already paid a fee up front in order to receive prescriptions. It means you do not have to pay every time you go to collect a prescription.

  4. Even if none of the above reasons (1-3) apply to you, you might be able to tell the NHS that you have exceptional reasons not to pay.

    5. What are exceptional reasons?

    An exceptional reason could be a number of things. For example:

    • English is not your first language so you were genuinely confused as to which box to tick on the prescription. You should send a copy of benefit letter- clarify with Sharan.
    • You could not read the prescription form due to illiteracy.
    • You suffer with a mental health condition which affected your ability to understand which box to tick on the prescription.
    • You were on Contribution-based Employment Support Allowance (ESA) but it was unreasonable for you to understand whether you were on Contribution-based ESA or Income Related ESA.
